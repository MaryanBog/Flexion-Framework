## 4.6 Divergence Angle $\Theta(X)$
The divergence angle describes the directional instability:

# 6. The Divergent Loop (Δ → Φ → M → κ → Δ)

\[
X_\Delta \;\rightarrow\; X_\Phi \;\rightarrow\; X_M \;\rightarrow\; X_\kappa \;\rightarrow\; X_\Delta.
\]

Each arrow represents a mathematically defined destabilizing influence from one structural dimension to the next.  
Once initiated, the loop accelerates unless strong counterforces intervene.

---

## 6.1 Δ → Φ: Differentiation Drives Energetic Amplification

Runaway differentiation ($X_\Delta$) increases structural contrast and over-resolution.

- excessive cognitive gradients,

Energetic overload in $X_\Phi$ produces strong $\Delta\Phi$-waves.

- memory deformation accelerates,  
- topological continuity breaks,  
- structural traces become unstable.

Formally:

\[
\frac{\partial X_M}{\partial X_\Phi} > 0.
\]

Large $\Delta\Phi$-waves tear the topology of $X_M$, increasing fragmentation and reducing homological coherence.

---

## 6.3 M → κ: Memory Fragmentation Reduces Stability

As $X_M$ fragments, long-range connectivity disappears, destroying coherent structure.

\[
\frac{\partial X_\kappa}{\partial X_M} < 0.

Consequences:

As $X_\kappa$ drops, the system loses resistance to structural change.

\[

\frac{\partial X_\Delta}{\partial X_\kappa} < 0.

Once initiated, the loop accelerates:

1. Δ grows → pushes Φ  
2. Φ overloads → ruptures M  
3. M fragments → collapses κ

Over time:

- $X_\Delta$ becomes chaotic,  
- $X_\Phi$ becomes resonant,

The loop forms the **core mechanism** of Deflexionization.

  \[
  \Omega_\Phi > \Omega_{\text{safe}}.

  \[
  F_M > F_{\text{crit}}.
  \]

  \[
  X_\kappa < \kappa_{\text{min}}.
  \]

Once two or more conditions hold, the loop becomes self-sustaining.

## 6.7 Loop Collapse Outcome

The Divergent Loop inevitably drives the system toward:
